Copy Strategy: Natty Kay / Operation Hair

Prepared by: Andrei Givero, AG Consulting Date: May 17, 2026

Voice Profile

Who she is: A Bay Area celebrity hairstylist who built a 28K personal brand and a 10-year LLC through craft, relationships, and word of mouth. She did not get here by running Google Ads or posting SEO blogs. She got here by being very good at what she does and by having real people want to be in her chair.

How she talks: Direct, confident, warm. She says "im interested" not "I am interested." She is not corporate. She is not formal. She speaks the way someone who works with their hands and talks to clients face-to-face all day speaks: straight to the point, no performance, real.

What she cares about: Her craft, her clients, her brand. She probably has strong opinions about hair, about quality, about how clients should be treated. She is likely skeptical of marketing-speak and has been pitched before. The reason she replied is that the pitch was specific and showed Andrei had actually looked at her business.

Voice rules for all deliverables: - Write to Natalie, not to "salon owners." She is not a category. She is a person. - One sentence at a time. No corporate stacking. - Use "you" not "your business" unless referring to the salon specifically. - No "revolutionary," "game-changer," "unlock," "in today's landscape," "let's dive in." - No consecutive short sentences stacked to create fake momentum. - Sign-off: "- Andrei" (not "Best, Andrei Givero, Founder of AG Consulting, etc.")

Bombshell Lead

The strongest opening for any deliverable aimed at Natalie is the authority-vs-invisibility gap:

Version A (direct): "You are one of the best-known celebrity hairstylists in the Bay Area. Limon Salon wins every AI search answer in San Jose. They have been in business 6 years less than you."

Version B (search-specific): "Five searches. 'Best celebrity hairstylist Bay Area.' 'Best hair extensions San Jose.' 'Best braids San Jose.' Operation Hair did not appear once. Limon Salon appeared in three of them. They have fewer celebrity credentials and more schema markup."

Version C (warm-reentry, post-reply): "You asked about my fee on May 15. Here is what I found first: zero of five AI searches in your market return Operation Hair. Limon Salon, Strictly Styles, and a Yelp aggregator take every answer. Your 131 Yelp reviews and 28K IG following are not the problem. The structural layer is."

Use Version C for the VSL and for any follow-up that references her reply. Version A for the hub landing page headline.

Positioning Angles

Angle 1: Celebrity authority, zero AI presence

She is #TheHairPlug. She has the celebrity positioning. She does not have the infrastructure for AI engines to cite her when a Bay Area woman types "best celebrity hairstylist near me." Limon Salon has half her personal brand and wins every answer because they have schema. This is a structural fix, not a marketing-spend fix.

Angle 2: 10-year foundation with no retention loop

Operation Hair LLC has been running since 2016. Ten years. And every client who leaves the chair leaves without an email address, a follow-up sequence, or a reason to rebook automatically. The revenue is entirely chair-throughput dependent. One email capture + a 5-email welcome flow changes the math.

Angle 3: 28K followers, no owned list

Her personal IG is the biggest asset in this pitch. 28K people follow Natalie specifically, not just the salon. None of them are on an email list. If IG algorithm shifts, or her account goes down, she loses all of it. One landing page with an email capture turns that audience into an asset she owns.

Angle 4: Specialist positioning not captured in search

She does celebrity hair, extensions, braids, locs, color, and wig installs for clients across San Jose and the broader Bay Area. None of those service lines have dedicated AEO-optimized pages with schema. Limon Salon has a page per service. She does not. Each service line is a separate keyword cluster that could be returning her as the answer.

Tone Calibration Per Deliverable

Deliverable Tone
Hub page headline Bold, confident, specific numbers (0/5 AEO)
Email sequence Warm, direct, salon-owner-to-client voice
VSL script Conversational, like Andrei talking to Natalie directly, not performing
DM 12-18 words, value upfront, zero wedge teaser
Proposal Businesslike but human; facts-first, no fluff

Key Phrases to Use (Andrei's Voice)

  • "I found that..." (discovery framing)
  • "The problem is structural" (not "you are doing it wrong")
  • "That is a closeable gap" (action framing)
  • "None of it is readable to AI search" (the wedge)
  • "You already have the authority" (credibility acknowledgment)

Copy Rules Checklist
