Content Strategy: Natty Kay / Operation Hair

Prepared by: Andrei Givero, AG Consulting Date: May 17, 2026 Platform priority: Instagram (her actual channel, 28K followers personal + salon account)

Content Pillars

Four pillars built around what her audience already follows her for, plus one that feeds the funnel.

Pillar 1: The Work (40% of posts)

Before/after transformations. Extension installs. Braid sets. Color results. Wig installs. This is her proof of craft. Each post is a mini-portfolio piece that drives follows and DMs. Format: Carousel or Reel showing process + final result.

Pillar 2: Celebrity & Clientele (20% of posts)

Behind-the-chair content featuring notable clients (cleared for public use only). On-set work if applicable. Name-drop moments when the client permits. This is the credibility layer that separates her from a standard salon IG. Format: Single photo or short Reel, minimal caption, client tag if permitted.

Pillar 3: Education & POV (25% of posts)

Her opinion on hair trends, technique tips, what to ask your stylist before extensions, how to maintain braids at home, extension myths. This positions her as the expert, not just a service provider. This pillar also feeds email capture: "Full guide in my bio link." Format: Talking-head Reel, carousel breakdown, or text-on-video.

Pillar 4: Salon Life + Behind the Scenes (15% of posts)

Day-in-the-life at Operation Hair, prep shots, product flat-lays, team moments if staff are present, client interactions (with permission). Human content. This builds parasocial connection and makes followers feel like regulars before they ever book. Format: Short Reel, Story save-worthy moment.

Short-Form Video Hooks (IG Reels)

Write these to open the first 1-3 seconds. No build-up. Value or pattern interrupt immediately.

Extension hooks: - "The number one thing stylists wish you knew before booking extensions." - "This is what bad extensions look like. Do not book this." - "I installed extensions on [CLIENT TYPE, e.g., a bride] three days before her wedding. Here is what happened." - "You have been sleeping on this extension method. Here is why."

Braid/natural hair hooks: - "Your braids are breaking your edges. Here is how to stop it." - "Three things your braider is not telling you." - "I have been braiding hair in San Jose for [X] years. This is what I see every week."

Celebrity/authority hooks: - "This is what it looks like when [CELEBRITY TYPE, e.g., a touring artist] calls the day before the show." [SWAP-SLOT -- only use if Natalie can name a cleared client or describe a real scenario] - "Not everyone who follows me knows I do celebrity hair. Here is one that did not expect me to share."

Education + funnel hooks: - "I made a full hair extension guide. It is free. Link in bio." - "Three signs you need to change your hairstylist." - "The question I get in my DMs more than any other."

Email Capture Strategy (to complement IG content)

Lead magnet options (pick one for launch): 1. "The Bay Area Hair Extension Guide: What to Ask, What to Pay, and What to Avoid" (PDF, gated by email) 2. "How to Make Your Braids Last 3 Months" (video or PDF guide) 3. "Natty Kay's Client Prep Checklist: How to Walk Into Your Appointment Ready"

Bio link setup: - Landing page at andrei170.github.io/natty-kay-operation-hair-hub/ links to booking + email capture - Single opt-in, first-name + email only, no friction - Immediate delivery of lead magnet, followed by welcome sequence (see email-sequence.md)

Content Execution Notes

  • Post Reels between 7-9am or 6-9pm Pacific. Her San Jose audience peaks in those windows.
  • Use location tags: "San Jose, CA" and "Bay Area" on every post. Geographic anchor is important for local discovery.
  • Do not use generic hashtags (#hair, #braids). Use specific: #SanJoseHair, #BayAreaHairstylist, #HairExtensionsSanJose, #TheHairPlug.
  • Captions: first line is the hook. Keep to 3-4 sentences max. CTA on last line (book, follow, or link in bio).
  • Stories are separate from feed. Use Stories for booking CTAs, polls, countdown timers to "last spot this week."
